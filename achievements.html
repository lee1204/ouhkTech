Student of Master of Philosophy receives Best Paper Award
17 Jul 2020

The IEEE Symposium on Computer Applications & Industrial Electronics (ISCAIE 2020) was held online on 18 – 19 April 2020. Chan Yin-hei, a student of Master of Philosophy from the School of Science and Technology, partnered with Prof. Andrew Lui Kwok-fai and Prof. Vanessa Ng Sin-chun, to write a paper entitled “A Neigbourhood Representation for Improving Diffusion based Short Term Traffic Flow Prediction”. Yin-hei presented the paper at the virtual symposium, which was recognized by the judges. He finally attained the Best Paper Award.

Computing trio wins first runner-up award at IEEE Competition
24 Aug 2019

A team of three final year computing students participated in the annual IEEE FYP Competition and brought home a first runner-up award with their research paper "Intelligent Traffic Light System for Hong Kong Traffic Network".
Their award-winning paper attempts to offer a solution to reduce traffic congestion in Hong Kong by designing an intelligent traffic light system to optimize traffic flow. With the data of road usage collected, the trio conducted a thorough analysis and successfully designed and developed an algorithm for an intelligent traffic light control system which can detect traffic flow in real time and switch traffic light signals to reduce average waiting time of pedestrians and vehicles. It is believed that their intelligent traffic light control system could increase the efficiency of the road space usage and improve the traffic condition in Hong Kong.

OU Computing students receive awards in algo trading competition
22 Dec 2018

A finance student Chan Ching-hsiang teamed up with an internet technology student Chan Ka-yan to participate in this year’s Computational Finance Competition. Leveraging on their respective professional knowledges and skills, the two OU students have successfully developed a computer trading programme which uses mathematical models to predict the local stock market movement. It allows users to know the best time to buy and sell stocks and gain maximum profit.
Among all the participants, the OU team has earned the largest positive profits and generated second highest risk-adjusted returns on the virtual trading platform with their algo trading software. The students were awarded the Best Overall Return Champion and the Best Risk-Adjusted Return First runner-up at the Award Presentation and Closing Ceremony held on 22 December at the City University of Hong Kong.

 
OU Computing students win championship in hackathon competition
19 Nov 2018

Three Year 4 computing students from the OU teamed up with a student from the Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU) and participated in the Classified Post Hackathon organized by the South China Morning Post. Among some 30 teams from various tertiary institutions, the "OU x BU" team won the championship and a cash prize of $8,000 with their creative digital solution.
Their winning solution is a role-playing game which simulates the various situations that people would face in their daily life. It is expected that through this game, people could have a better understanding of the relationship between insurance and daily life, and also learn more about the insurance industry.

 
OU team shines at international ICT competition
13 Oct 2018

Three computing students joined the Hong Kong ICT (Information and Communications Technology) Delegation and participated in the APICTA 2018, which is considered as the "Oscars of the ICT arena".
