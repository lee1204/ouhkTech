The First VR Game about Blood Donation in Hong Kong - Cooperate with Hong Kong Red Cross
2 Jun 2020

Final Year Project Team LetsGoParty, consist of three students, Yhin Yip Hin CHOI, Sunny Kin San LAW and Ben Tsz Hong WONG, supervised by Professor Andrew LUI, cooperated with Hong Kong Red Cross to design the first-ever VR Game about Blood Donation in Hong Kong for their final year project titled “Educate positive image for teenagers about blood donation with Virtual Reality”.

Dr.Lee, Director of Hong Kong Red Cross, and Chelly Chu, Nurse of Hong Kong Red Cross were invited to evaluate our VR game on the demonstration day. They are satisfied with the VR game. They are willing to use this VR game to bring a positive image to break the teenagers’ fear of blood drawing and strengthen their interest in blood donation to support the Red Cross blood drawing service in the future.

The Peer Tutoring Scheme has been established in the Computing Programmes Team since 2008/09. The computing programme offers 6 to 8 positions of Peer Tutors every year to final year students in BCOMPHITJ. Peer Tutors are honourable appointments and receive an hourly part-time job rate as Student Trainee.

Peer tutors are student leaders who are responsible for assisting the instruction work in the computing programme team. In most cases, they will be involved in classroom teaching and consultation under the supervision of an instructor. Sometimes they will be assigned to other duties such as clerical work and event helpers. They are regarded as part of the teaching team of OUHK Computing. The appointees will gain valuable experience at tertiary level instruction.

The past peer tutors developed strong leadership through the programme which is helpful to their career. You can read more about the programme here: computing.ouhk.edu.hk/web/student/PeerTutors

 
Two Projects Receive HK$200,000 Funding from Open InnoChallenge (OIC) Sponsorship
23 Sep 2019

Open InnoChallenge (OIC) is a brand-new endeavor launched in 2017 organized by Student Affairs Office (SAO) aiming to unleash students’ creativity and innovation, and enhance students’ entrepreneurial skills to translate the innovative ideas “into viable operation” for profit or for social good. It strives to inspire students’ innovation via “Experience” and “Empower” in the way of out-of-the-classroom learning. It encourages students to develop a feasible proposal for a startup enterprise with funding of $100,000.

This year, two teams of computing graduates have successfully obtained a start-up fund of HK$100,000 for their awarded projects, Banana Portal and Fruit Peel Family, in order to carry out their business plan. The Open InnoCelebration was also held on 21 September 2019, an Awards Presentation Ceremony cum Exhibition of Open InnoChallenge which OU students and alumni demonstrate their innovative projects. Apart from creative exhibition booths, OU students will also host a series of interactive workshops to promote environmental protection and emotional health.

Website: www.ouhk.edu.hk/sao/oic

Two Students Participated in the Youth Internship Programme at the Chinese Academy of Sciences
27 Jun 2019

In the middle of June, two students from the computing programme were admitted to the Youth Internship Programme at the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS) for six weeks, conducting science and technology research such as artificial intelligence, intelligent robots, and autonomous systems in the Institute of Automation.

The programme is organised by the Home Affairs Bureau and co-organised by the academy and the Hong Kong Volunteers Association. Under the six-week internship, 49 tertiary students from Hong Kong have been assigned to research units under the academy in areas such as artificial intelligence, intelligent robots, autonomous vehicles, mathematics, physics and life sciences. A number of research units including the Institute of Automation, the Institute of Computing Technology, the Institute of Software, the Institute of Mathematics, the Institute of Physics and the Institute of Microbiology are involved.

Vice President Professor Kwan Ching Ping Reggie was also invited to attend the opening ceremony of the Youth Internship Programme at the Chinese Academy of Sciences.

PwC HackaDay 2019
20 Jun 2019

Congratulations to the two OU Computing teams, 4LS ranked third, TEAM ranked sixth, among the 12 local and Macau universities in the PwC HackaDay. HackaDay is a Capture the Flag cybersecurity intercollegiate hacking competition organised by PricewaterhouseCoopers annually. It is aimed at increasing the general awareness of the importance of cybersecurity amongst youths in Hong Kong and Macau, who will eventually become the pillars of our society. HackaDay also serves as a platform to raise the competency level of new talents to better prepare them for a meaningful career in cybersecurity. Students are required to exploit various security vulnerabilities in four categories: Web Applications, Network, Binary, and Blockchain within six hours.

 
The 44th International Collegiate Programming Contest (ICPC) Asia Nanchang Regional Contest
10 Jun 2019

Last weekend, three computing final-year students, supervised by assistant professor Dr. Keith Lee, were invited to the 44th International Collegiate Programming Contest (ICPC) Asia Nanchang Regional Contest held at Jiangxi Normal University, China. They formed a team of three, representing the Open University of Hong Kong, worked to solve the most real-world programming problems within just 5 hours. Through training and competition, students are able to foster teamwork, creativity, innovation in building new software programs, and enables students to test their ability to perform under pressure.

The International Collegiate Programming Contest is an algorithmic programming contest for college students. It is the oldest, largest, and most prestigious programming contest in the world. The contest has raised aspirations and performance of generations of the world’s problem solvers in the computing sciences and engineering.

Thanks for the "Katie Shu Sui Pui Charitable Trust - We Can Fly" project and Jiangxi Normal University for sponsoring the registration fee for this contest.
